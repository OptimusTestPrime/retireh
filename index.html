Assumptions & Methodology

This tool models your retirement by running a year-by-year simulation based on standard Canadian rules and your economic inputs.

One-Time Events (Windfalls & Expenses)

  • Multiple Events: Model up to 10 specific life events. Use negative numbers for planned large purchases. The model pulls expenses out of your Non-Registered savings first.
  • Principal Residence Exemption: In Canada, the sale of your primary home is generally exempt from capital gains taxes. We deposit positive windfalls directly into your Non-Registered account without triggering a tax event.
  • Inheritances: Canadian inheritances are modeled as tax-free cash injections, as taxes are handled by the estate prior to distribution.

Government Benefits (CPP & OAS)

  • CPP (Canada Pension Plan): Can be started between age 60 and 70. Starting early permanently reduces your pension by 7.2% per year before 65. Delaying increases it by 8.4% per year after 65. We assume an average base payout of ~$11,500/year (today's dollars) at age 65.
  • OAS (Old Age Security): Can be started between age 65 and 70. Delaying increases the payout by 7.2% per year after 65. We assume a full base payout of ~$9,100/year (today's dollars) at age 65.
  • OAS Clawback (Recovery Tax): If your taxable income exceeds the OAS threshold (~$95,000 in today's dollars), the government claws back 15% of the excess, up to the full amount of your OAS. Our engine accurately models this. It dynamically indexes the $95k threshold to inflation and automatically calculates the 15% penalty if your taxable withdrawals cross the line, grossing up your savings withdrawals to cover the difference.

Employer Pension Rules

  • Federal (PSSA): Applies a 5% penalty per year if you collect before age 60 (Group 1) or 65 (Group 2), unless you have 30 years of service. An estimated bridge benefit is paid until age 65.
  • Provincial (85 Factor): Applies a 3% penalty for every year you are short of reaching 85 points (Age + Years of Service) or age 65.

Taxation & Withdrawal Strategy

The engine calculates the "Gross" amount you need to withdraw to pay the CRA and hit your target Net income:

  • Account Treatment:
    • Pensions, CPP, OAS, and RRSP/RRIF: Treated as 100% taxable regular income.
    • TFSA: Treated as 100% tax-free.
    • Non-Registered: Approximated as 25% taxable (assuming half tax-free principal and half capital gains at a 50% inclusion rate).
  • Tax Brackets: The model uses an estimated blended Fed/Prov tax profile, applying a tax-free Basic Personal Amount (~$15,500). These brackets are indexed to your inflation rate yearly to prevent artificial "bracket creep."
