Assumptions & Methodology

This tool models your retirement by running a year-by-year simulation based on standard Canadian rules and your economic inputs.

Staggered Retirement (Transition Phase)

If one partner retires before the other, the simulation enters a transition phase. The working partner's "Current Net Salary" is applied to directly offset the household's desired income goal. Their RRSP/TFSA contributions also continue until they reach their designated retirement age.

Federal Pension (PSSA) Logic

  • Bridge Benefit: Paid from retirement until age 65. It represents the portion of your pension that is replaced by CPP.
  • Group 1 (Before 2013): Unreduced pension at age 60, or age 55 with 30 years of service. Otherwise, a 5% penalty applies per year early.
  • Group 2 (2013 or Later): Unreduced pension at age 65, or age 60 with 30 years of service. Otherwise, a 5% penalty applies per year early.

Government Benefits (CPP & OAS)

  • CPP: Starts between age 60 and 70. Starting early permanently reduces your pension by 7.2% per year before 65. Delaying increases it by 8.4% per year after 65. Base assumed: ~$11,500/year at 65.
  • OAS: Starts between age 65 and 70. Delaying increases the payout by 7.2% per year after 65. Base assumed: ~$9,100/year at 65.
  • OAS Clawback: If your taxable income exceeds the OAS threshold (~$95,000 indexed to inflation), the government claws back 15% of the excess. This engine automatically calculates and pays this penalty.
