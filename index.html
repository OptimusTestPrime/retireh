Assumptions & Methodology

⚠️ IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER: This tool is for educational and illustrative purposes only. It does not constitute professional financial, tax, or legal advice. Canadian tax laws and pension regulations are subject to change and vary significantly by individual circumstances. Please consult with a certified financial planner or qualified tax professional before making any financial decisions.

1. Economic Assumptions

  • Inflation: Fixed at 2.2% annually. We index your income goal and government benefits to this rate to maintain purchasing power.
  • Growth Rates: 6% annual return on savings while working; 4% annual return during retirement (conservative shift).
  • CPP & OAS: Combined estimate of ~$21,000/year (today's dollars) starting at age 65.

2. Pension Calculations (Bridge & Penalties)

  • PSSA (Federal): Uses the 2% formula. If retiring early, a 5% reduction per year is applied for every year you are short of age 60 (Group 1) or age 65 (Group 2), unless the 30-year service rule is met. A bridge benefit is paid until age 65.
  • 85-Factor (Provincial): Pension is unreduced if Age + Years of Service ≥ 85. Otherwise, a 3% reduction per year is applied based on the shorter gap to the 85-factor or age 65.

3. Tax & Withdrawal Logic

  • Tax Engine: A simplified "Gross-Up" model that estimates federal and provincial income taxes to ensure your "Net" goal is met.
  • Priority: We prioritize spending Pensions first, then Non-Registered savings, then RRSPs, and finally TFSAs to minimize the immediate tax burden.
